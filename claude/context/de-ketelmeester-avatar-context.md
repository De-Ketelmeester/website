# De Ketelmeester - Customer Avatar, Desires & Pain Points Context

## Purpose of this document

This document captures the target customer avatar for De Ketelmeester's emergency CV-ketel Google Ads and landing page strategy.

Use this document as context for future LLM instances when creating:
- Google Ads copy
- Landing page copy
- Keyword strategy
- Negative keyword strategy
- Ad group structure
- Conversion-focused messaging
- Customer research summaries
- Sales scripts or phone call scripts

The goal is to keep all future marketing work focused on the highest-intent customer: someone with an urgent CV-ketel problem who is ready to contact a technician now.

## 2. Core customer avatar

## The Stressed Homeowner With an Urgent CV Problem

This is the primary avatar for De Ketelmeester's emergency CV campaign.

They are a homeowner, renter, partner, parent, or household decision-maker whose CV-ketel suddenly stops working or causes an urgent household problem.

They are usually dealing with one of these situations:
- No heating
- No hot water
- A CV-ketel storing
- A broken CV-ketel
- A leaking CV-ketel
- A CV system that does not work properly
- An urgent need for a CV-monteur

They are not browsing casually. They want help quickly.

### Core avatar statement

De Ketelmeester helps homeowners and residents who suddenly have a CV-ketel problem, such as no heating, no hot water, a storing, a broken ketel, or a leak. They are stressed, want fast certainty, and prefer to call a reliable 24/7 technician who can help quickly.

## 3. Who this person is

The ideal customer is usually:

- A homeowner or resident in one of the service regions
- Searching from home, often on mobile
- Experiencing a practical household problem right now
- Annoyed, cold, stressed, or in a hurry
- Not technically knowledgeable
- Not looking for a long explanation
- Not primarily looking for DIY advice
- Ready to call if the ad and landing page immediately match their problem
- Looking for trust, speed, and certainty

### Awareness level

This person is mostly problem-aware or solution-aware.

They already know there is a problem and are looking for help.

Common mindset:
- "Mijn CV doet het niet."
- "Ik heb geen warm water."
- "Mijn ketel heeft storing."
- "Ik heb snel een monteur nodig."
- "Kan iemand dit vandaag oplossen?"

## 5. Biggest desires

## Desire 1: They want the problem fixed today

Their main desire is speed and certainty. They want to know someone can answer the phone, understand the problem, and help quickly.

### Message angle

CV storing? Bel direct. 24/7 bereikbaar. Snel een monteur.

### Why this matters

This customer is in an urgent situation. A slow, generic, or overly informative message will underperform compared with direct emergency-focused messaging.

---

## Desire 2: They want heating and hot water back

The real outcome is not "CV service." The real outcome is comfort at home.

They want:
- A warm house again
- Hot water again
- A normal shower
- A functioning household
- Less stress

### Message angle

Geen warm water of verwarming? Direct hulp bij CV-storing.

---

## Desire 3: They want to speak to a real person

Emergency home-service customers often prefer calling because it gives immediate certainty.

They want to know:
- Is someone available?
- Can someone help today?
- What should I do now?
- Can I book a technician directly?

### Message angle

Bel direct onze storingsdienst. Snel duidelijkheid.

- Experience with known CV brands such as Nefit, Remeha, Honeywell, Inventum, ATAG, and Vaillant

### Message angle

Ervaren CV-monteurs. 24/7 bereikbaar. Hulp bij storing en reparatie.

## 6. Biggest pain points

## Pain point 1: No heating or no hot water

This is the strongest emotional pain. It affects comfort immediately, especially in the morning, evening, winter, or when there are children in the home.

### Customer thoughts

- "Ik heb geen warm water."
- "Het huis wordt koud."
- "Ik kan niet douchen."
- "Dit moet snel opgelost worden."

### Best response in copy

Geen verwarming of warm water? Bel direct voor hulp bij CV-storing.

---

## Pain point 2: Panic because they do not know what is wrong

They may not know whether the issue is dangerous, expensive, simple, or serious.

### Customer thoughts

- "Is mijn ketel kapot?"
- "Is dit gevaarlijk?"
- "Moet ik de ketel vervangen?"
- "Kan dit snel gerepareerd worden?"

### Best response in copy

Onze monteur kijkt snel wat er mis is en helpt direct bij CV-storingen.

---

## Pain point 3: Fear of waiting too long

They are often worried that nobody will answer, especially in the evening, weekend, or outside office hours.

### Customer thoughts

- "Kan iemand vandaag komen?"
- "Is er een spoedmonteur beschikbaar?"
- "Word ik wel teruggebeld?"
- "Kan ik direct iemand spreken?"

### Best response in copy

24/7 bereikbaar voor CV-storingen. Bel direct voor snelle hulp.

---

## Pain point 4: Fear of unclear or high costs

Even urgent customers still worry about price. They may fear call-out fees, weekend charges, unnecessary replacements, or unclear repair costs.

### Customer thoughts

- "Wat gaat dit kosten?"
- "Word ik niet opgelicht?"
- "Krijg ik eerst duidelijkheid?"
- "Moet mijn ketel meteen vervangen worden?"

### Best response in copy

Bel direct voor snelle duidelijkheid en hulp van een ervaren CV-monteur.

---

## Pain point 5: Distrust of low-quality technicians

Emergency service markets can feel risky. Customers may have had bad experiences with no-shows, poor communication, or companies that push unnecessary work.

### Customer thoughts

- "Komt er wel een goede monteur?"
- "Is dit bedrijf betrouwbaar?"
- "Hebben ze ervaring met mijn ketel?"
- "Wordt het probleem goed opgelost?"

### Best response in copy

VCA-gecertificeerde monteurs met 30+ jaar ervaring.

---

## Pain point 6: They do not want DIY content

This avatar is generally not looking for a tutorial, forum, cause explanation, or step-by-step repair guide.

Research-heavy wording is weaker for the emergency campaign.

- resetten

- wat te doen

These searches may be useful for SEO or content marketing, but they are usually poor fits for a small-budget emergency Google Ads campaign.

## 9. Messaging hierarchy

Use this order in ads and landing page copy.

### 1. Problem match

Immediately mirror the user's issue.

Examples:
- CV storing?
- Geen warm water?
- Geen verwarming?
- CV-ketel kapot?
- CV-ketel lekt?

### 2. Urgency

Make it clear that help is available quickly.

Examples:
- 24/7 bereikbaar
- Spoedservice
- Bel direct
- Snel een monteur

### 3. Outcome

Describe the result they want.

Examples:
- Snel weer warm water
- Verwarming werkt weer
- Probleem snel opgelost
- Minder stress, snel duidelijkheid

### 4. Trust

Add proof and credibility.

Examples:
- 30+ jaar ervaring
- VCA-gecertificeerde monteurs
- Ervaring met bekende CV-merken
- Betrouwbare storingsdienst

### 5. Action

Make the next step obvious.

Examples:
- Bel direct
- Neem direct contact op
- Vraag direct hulp aan
- Plan snel een monteur

## 14. Copywriting rules for future LLMs

When writing for this avatar:

1. Start with the exact problem.
2. Use urgent, direct language.
3. Keep the call-to-action phone focused.
4. Mention 24/7 availability early.
5. Use simple Dutch, not technical jargon.
6. Mention trust proof after the problem and CTA.
7. Avoid long explanations above the fold.
8. Avoid making the customer think too much.
9. Avoid generic service claims without problem match.
10. Do not mix emergency CV intent with maintenance, installation, or general plumbing.

## 15. Best reusable summary block

De Ketelmeester's best Google Ads avatar is a homeowner or resident with an urgent CV-ketel problem. They may have no heating, no hot water, a CV storing, a broken CV-ketel, or a leaking CV-ketel. They are stressed, often searching on mobile, and want fast certainty from a reliable technician. Avoid installation, maintenance, boiler-only, fixture-level, DIY, and research-heavy intent unless a separate campaign is created.
